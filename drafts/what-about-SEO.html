What about SEO

Mon 25 September 2017      Marie

If you have read my previous articles, you probably know that I created my blog with a static content generator. It means that I do not use WordPress or any other dynamic content management system.

Since I use Pelican and it do not have a SEO plugin available, I have to generate my own meta tags to have a great web ranking.

If you would like to learn more about SEO and figure out how to generate more traffic on you website, then this article might help you.

What is SEO

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. In other words, SEO is a bunch of techniques to upgrade your website ranking. Ranking? Yes, you know how some websites get to the top of google research's page when you research specific keywords.

There are two kinds of ranking, the organic or natural one and the paid one. Organic ranking is basically earned by adding some HTML tags to your pages, adding or getting appropriate relations between links and creating original and cohesive content. SEO is all about growing visibility in an organic way.

How to

Since SEO is a matter of orienting search engines in the right direction, we will have to talk their language. To do so, we will play with HTML. First, it is important to know that all of your tags must be listed in between the <head> and </head> tags of your HTML page.

Titles

There's a lot of tags available to provide information about your website. The first and probably the most basic and important one would be the <title>. The content of this tag will appear at the top of your browser in a tab. Also, search engines use it when it comes to showing your page as a result. To be clearer, it provides a title to the page and represents what is in the page. For example, if your page is an article, you should show the title of the article, if it is a contact page, the title should be something like contact me... To apply this tag, you would have to put the following line in your HTML code: 

<head>
<title>MY WEBSITE NAME - MY ARTICLE TITLE OR MY PAGE TITLE</title>
</head>

Keywords and description

Following the title, other necessary tags would be the description that will provide a description when your website appears on a result page of a search engine and keywords that will describe your website allowing people to find you when typing those keywords in their search engine. To apply them to your site you would have to add this line: 

<meta name="TAG_NAME" content="THE_CONTENT"/>

Here is an example: 

<head>
<meta name="description" content="This is a website about technology."/>
<meta name="keywords" content="technology, blog"/>
</head>

Links

Another particular thing to think about is guiding search engines through URLs! In other words, google sends robots to look into every links and pages your website contains and reference them. To direct those robots to good places, we will have to tell them with a simple meta tag named robots.

This tag takes two arguments - index or noindex This means do you want this page to be indexed by the search engine. (Normally you want it to be indexed). - follow or nofollow That means do you want the robot to follow all the links on this page. (Normally you want it to be followed, if it's an external link then maybe you don't want it to be followed because it refers to another website.)
Note: It is possible to only follow selected links by adding an attribute rel="nofollow" to all the links you do not want the search engine to follow.

When you have made your choices, then you can add the meta tag to your website like this: 

<meta name="robots" content="index, follow"/>

Sometimes you might add links to an article, but it

It might comes a time where you have duplicated content on different URL. This could happen if you use a CMS with unfriendly URL or if you start posting your articles on other websites. To avoid bad ranking, you should add a link meta data containing the original content URL. To do so, you have to add this tag: 

<link rel="canonical" href="THE_ORIGINAL_CONTENT_URL" />

Another nice meta tag is the revisit-after tag. With this meta tag, you can specify to robots to come back and visit your website after x days or months. So if you know that you will post every 7 days, you can now have your content indexed as soon as this. Here is how you do it:

<meta name="revisit-after" content="X days">

Schemas

Another trick to upgrade your SEO to the next level would be to add schema to your data. Schema is a way to provide more information about you pages. For example, you can create a schema of your article. This part of SEO will be added in between <body> and </body> tags. Here is what it would look like

<article itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting" itemprop="blogPost">
<h1 itemprop="headline">The title</h1>
<span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person" itemprop="author">
    <span> itemprop="name">the author's name</span>
</span>
<div itemprop="articleBody">
Here is the content of the article.
</div>
</article>

You can do more than add simple article. It is possible to declare all of your blog with it. To do so, I recommand reading a little more about it on the schema website.

Is there a simpler way to SEO?

Depending on your Content Management System, you will find plugins that can help you with your SEO. WordPress plugins library has a nice SEO plugin named Yoast. Middleman also has a SEO tool that I have not tested and you can find it here. Concercing Pelican, I did not find a SEO plugin for it so I had to write lines myself in order to add some nice referencing to my website.
